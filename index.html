QUEENSSCRIPTORIUM

Can the NYPD Make Better Use of Proposed 116th Precinct?

By Hiram Alejandro Duran

Queens’ Community District 13 residents are overjoyed as they clear the last few hurdles on their path to the construction of a new NYPD precinct. The 116th precinct comes decades after local residents first started petitioning city officials to lessen slow response times.

“We can expect a ‘shovel in the ground’ by Spring next year,” said District Manager Mark McMillan as he jubilantly mimed the action ahead of a Public Safety Committee meeting.

However, data collected from NYC Citywide Agency Performance Mapping shows other, smaller, districts in Queens have perennially faced longer response times. Districts’ 8, served by 107th, and 10, served by 106th, average the longest response times at over six and a half minutes.

Since 2012, all precincts but the 111th have experienced parabola like ups-and-downs in response times. (Quote from expert of NYPD but really DCPI on the cause of parabola like ups-and-downs in Queens’ precincts response times).

The new precinct is expected to half the 12.6-mile district currently being served by the 105th, lead by Commanding Officer Netais Gilbert, into north and south patrol areas.
